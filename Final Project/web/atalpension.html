Atal Pension Yojana (APY)


Atal Pension Yojana (APY)
Launched: 9 May 2015
Main Objective: Increase the number of people covered under any kind of pension scheme. Atal Pension Yojana is one of the three Jan Suraksha schemes launched by PM Narendra Modi. APY is aimed at increasing the number of pension scheme beneficiaries across the country. The scheme is especially targeted to the private unorganized sector and is open to all Indian citizens between the age of 18 to 40 years. Under the scheme, the beneficiary have to make contribution for at least 20 years before he/she can get pension after attaining age of 60 years. The scheme provides a monthly pension of Rs 1000 to Rs. 5000 per month based on the contribution amount.
Official Website: http://www.jansuraksha.gov.in

Benefits of Atal Pension Yojna
• Central Government guaranteed minimum pension amount : Each beneficiary under APY shall receive a Central Government guaranteed minimum pension of Rs. 1000 per month or Rs. 2000 per month or Rs. 3000 per month or Rs. 4000 per month or Rs. 5000 per month, after the age of 60 years until death. • The age of stop contribution and start getting pension is 60 years. • Return of the pension wealth to the nominee of the subscriber: After the demise of both the subscriber and the spouse, the nominee of the subscriber receive the pension wealth, as accumulated till age 60 of the subscriber. • Central Government guaranteed minimum pension amount to the spouse. In case of beneficiaries accidental death his/her family would get pension.
Eligibility for getting benefits of Atal Pension Yojna
1. Atal Pension Yojana (APY) is open to all bank account holders. 2. The minimum age of joining APY is 18 years and maximum age is 40 years. 3. Minimum period of contribution by the subscriber under APY should be 20 years or more. 4. The beneficiary should not be the tax payer. 5. The beneficiary should not covered under any required social security scheme.
