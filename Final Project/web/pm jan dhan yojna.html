Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY)


Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY)
Launched: 28 August 2014
Main Objective: Financial inclusion and access to financial services for all households in the country. Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY) is a national mission to bring comprehensive financial inclusion of all the households in the country. Under the PMJDY, any individual above the age of 10 years and does not ave a bank account can open a bank account without depositing any money. The scheme was to ensure the access to financial services such as banking / savings & deposit Accounts, remittance, credit, debit cards, insurance and pension in affordable manner. The scheme was mostly targeted to the people belonging to the Below Poverty Line but is beneficial to everyone who does not have a bank account. Jan Dhan Yojana has seen a great success, about 21 Crore accounts have been opened in just about one and half year under the scheme. Out of the total 12.87 crore in rural area and 8.13 Crore accounts have been opened in urban areas. Despite of zero minimum balance, there is 33074.89 crore rupees balance in these accounts with 28.88% accounts opened with zero balance.
Official Website: http://www.pmjdy.gov.in

Pradhan Mantri Jan-Dhan Yojna (PMJDY) is the mission launched by Mr. Narendra Modi Prime Minister of India in the year 2014 on his first Independence Day speech. The purpose of starting this missions is to make banking services like banking savings and deposit accounts, debit/credit card services, remittance, insurance, pension available for the poor people as well. Under this scheme users are allowed to open a bank account with zero balance and are granted RuPay debit card. Being banking services made available so easily Pradhan Mantri Jan-Dhan Yojna created Guinness world record of opening most number of bank accounts in the time period of one week. Another big achievement is that by Feb 10, 2016, 200 million bank accounts are being opened under this scheme and Rs. 323.78 billion are deposited. The scheme became a massive success to banking industry.
Benefits of Pradhan Mantri Jan-Dhan Yojna
• Zero Balance account: Under this scheme users are allowed to open a bank account with no balance in it. • Debit Card service: Even with zero balance account holder is given the service of debit card by RuPay. • Accidental Death insurance: In case of accidental death the nominee of the account holder will get insurance of Rs. 1, 00,000. • Life insurance cover: All accounts opened by 26th January 2015 are given additional life insurance of Rs. 30,000. • Overdraft allowed: After six months of account opening holder can overdraft up to Rs. 5,000. • Online Banking: With digitization even all account in Pradhan Mantri Jan-Dhan Yojna are also allowed to enjoy online banking facilities.
Eligibility for Pradhan Mantri Jan-Dhan Yojna:
1. Any person with Indian nationality is eligible for Jan-Dhan Yojna 2. Any person of age 10 years is eligible but minors need to have guardian to manage their account. 3. If person do not have any proof of nationality but bank on their research finds that person is Indian, he/she is also eligible for the scheme. 4. Any person already having savings account with nationalized bank can transfer his/her account into Pradhan Mantri Jan-Dhan Yojna and can enjoy the benefits.
